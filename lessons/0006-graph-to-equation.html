Lesson 0006 · CLEP Precalculus

Graph ↔ Equation

Lesson 0003 taught the four knobs one at a time. The exam never asks them one at a time — it hands you a curve and four equations that each get three features right.

Every wrong answer choice on a graph-matching question is built the same way: correct amplitude and period, wrong phase — or correct everything except a missing reflection. If you read features one at a time and stop at the first match, you'll pick a distractor that agrees with you on three counts. The fix is a fixed reading order ending in a verification step: plug in one x-value and check.

Primary source for this lesson: Paul's Online Math Notes — Graphs of Trig Functions, plus your sinusoids reference, which now includes the recipe below.

The reading order

  1. Midline first: D = (max + min)/2. Draw it mentally through the curve.
  2. Amplitude: |A| = (max − min)/2.
  3. Period → B: measure one full cycle, then B = 2π/period.
  4. Anchor a feature for phase and sign together: find a maximum (cleanest cos anchor) or an upward midline crossing (cleanest sin anchor). Where that feature sits determines C — and whether you need a reflection.
  5. Verify with one plug-in. Evaluate your equation at one labeled x-value. This step is what kills the three-out-of-four distractors.
Why phase and reflection must be read together They're interchangeable disguises: −sin x = sin(x − π), and cos x = sin(x + π/2). The same curve has many correct equations, so you can't read "is there a minus sign?" off the picture — you can only anchor a feature and check. CLEP exploits this by offering a reflected form and a shifted form of almost the same thing, exactly one of which matches.
