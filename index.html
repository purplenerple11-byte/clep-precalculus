CLEP Precalculus

Trigonometry

Short, interactive lessons built around one exam. Each one gives a single tangible win you can build on.

Trigonometry is officially 15% of the CLEP Precalculus score, but College Board states that right-triangle trig or the properties of trig functions actually drive 30–40% of the questions once you count the trig embedded in other topics. (Official exam page) That's why these lessons lead with it.

Lessons

0001 Applying Trig Identities Moving from "I recognise that identity" to "I know which one to reach for." 0002 Solving Right Triangles SOHCAHTOA, angles of elevation, and inverse trig — anchored to the unit circle you already know. 0003 Graphing Sinusoids Amplitude, period, phase shift and midline — four independent knobs, and the two couplings CLEP hides traps in. 0004 Inverse Trig & the Principal Range Why sin⁻¹(sin 2π/3) ≠ 2π/3 — evaluating arcsin, arccos, arctan and landing in the right window. 0005 The Triangle Inside the Ratio Given sin θ = z/10, recover everything else — the blueprint move behind cos(arcsin x) and quadrant-sign questions.
