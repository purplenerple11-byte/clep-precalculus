Trig Identities

Condensed from Paul's Online Math Notes — Trig Cheat Sheet. Print this; keep it next to you while drilling identity-application problems.

Pythagorean identities

IdentityRearranged forms (handy for substitution)
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1sin²θ = 1 − cos²θ  ·  cos²θ = 1 − sin²θ
1 + tan²θ = sec²θtan²θ = sec²θ − 1
1 + cot²θ = csc²θcot²θ = csc²θ − 1

Reciprocal & quotient identities

csc θ = 1/sin θsec θ = 1/cos θcot θ = 1/tan θ
tan θ = sin θ / cos θcot θ = cos θ / sin θ

Co-function identities (radians)

sin(π/2 − θ) = cos θcos(π/2 − θ) = sin θ
tan(π/2 − θ) = cot θcot(π/2 − θ) = tan θ

Sum & difference identities

sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B
cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B
tan(A ± B) = (tan A ± tan B) / (1 ∓ tan A tan B)
Sign pattern Sine keeps the sign match (± → ±). Cosine flips it (± → ∓). Say it out loud a few times — it's the #1 source of sign errors.

Double-angle identities

sin(2θ) = 2 sin θ cos θ
cos(2θ) = cos²θ − sin²θ = 2cos²θ − 1 = 1 − 2sin²θ
tan(2θ) = 2tan θ / (1 − tan²θ)
Why three forms of cos(2θ)? Pick the form that eliminates the variable you don't have. Solving for cos θ alone? Use 2cos²θ − 1. Solving for sin θ alone? Use 1 − 2sin²θ.

Half-angle identities

sin(θ/2) = ±√[(1 − cos θ)/2]
cos(θ/2) = ±√[(1 + cos θ)/2]
tan(θ/2) = (1 − cos θ)/sin θ = sin θ/(1 + cos θ)

Sign of the ± is decided by which quadrant θ/2 lands in — not by the sign of θ.
