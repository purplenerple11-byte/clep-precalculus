The relativity rule The hypotenuse never changes — it's always opposite the right angle. Opposite and adjacent swap when you switch reference angles. Always name sides after choosing your angle, never before.

Which ratio do I use?

Two knowns, one unknown. Find the row that mentions both the side you have and the side you want.

Direction of travel

Angle in → ratio outsin, cos, tan
Ratio in → angle outsin⁻¹, cos⁻¹, tan⁻¹
Notation trap sin⁻¹θ is the inverse sine. It is not 1/sin θ — that's csc θ. CLEP baits this deliberately.

Pattern, not brute memory Sine runs √0/2, √1/2, √2/2, √3/2, √4/2 across the row. Cosine is the same list backwards. If you blank on a value, rebuild the row — it's faster than panicking.

Applied angle vocabulary

Angle of elevationmeasured up from the horizontal to the line of sight
Angle of depressionmeasured down from the horizontal to the line of sight

Both are measured from the horizontal, never the vertical. In a two-observer figure, the angle of depression from the top equals the angle of elevation from the bottom — alternate interior angles across parallel horizontals.

Setup checklist

  1. Draw it; mark the right angle (word problems hide it — "vertical," "level ground," "plumb").
  2. Label the givens; circle the target.
  3. Name sides relative to your chosen angle.
  4. Pick the ratio mentioning both known and wanted.
  5. Solve, then sanity-check.
